More information about the woman:

She led an intriguing life that was almost as mysterious as the stories she wrote. She was born in 1890 in England, and soon developed a passion for storytelling and writing at a young age. Her love for adventure took her to different places, including a trip to Egypt where she gained inspiration for some of her most famous novels. In 1914, she got married, but their marriage faced challenges, leading to their separation. During WWI, she worked as a nurse- a position that provided her with inspiration for some of her books she wrote in her time there. However, it was her debut novel, "The Mysterious Affair at Styles," featuring the brilliant detective Hercule Poirot, that truly launched her career. She went on to write numerous captivating mysteries, and her talent for crafting clever plots and intriguing characters made her one of the most successful authors in history. To this day, readers all over the world enjoy her novels, making her a literary legend.
